ADACS astrocomp week 2020

Sydney, February 2020

Inspired by astrohackweek and Scicoder we are organising a week long coding workshop and uncoference for Australia

About astrocomp

In early 2020 ADACS is planning to run a week of computational workshops focussed on PhD students and ECR.

The content and workshop is developed in collaboration with ANITA, OzGrav, ASTRO 3D, and the ASVOs with input from the ECR chapter and PhD students. See the SOC peage for more info.
This workshop will build on workshops offered by ADACS in previous years as well as incorporate elements from workshops such as Python in Astronomy and SciCoder and the astrohackweek.

The Python in Astronomy meeting includes unconference sessions, presentations, sprints and tutorials. The content of the meeting is largely dependent on the attendees who are given the opportunity to shape the meeting to their needs, this ensures the attendees are highly engaged.
The SciCoder workshop is designed to introduce modern programming practices, languages, and tools as specifically applicable to scientific research. It is not an introduction to programming, but targets those who have not had much (or any) formal training in writing software and who would like focussed training on software development.
Astrohackweek is a week long workshop that combines summer school and unconference: http://astrohackweek.org/2019/.

WE BELIEVE IN GREAT IDEAS

For this workshop we aim to bring together attendees with a wide variety of skill levels and we encourage everyone to share their knowledge and expertise with each other. Aside from structured tutorials in the mornings the workshop will include unconference sessions, presentations, sprints and participant led tutorials in the afternoons. As well as upskilling the participants the workshop aims to encourage collaboration and sharing of knowledge on astronomy tools, in particualr useuful Python packages, techniques and coding practices.
